## Immediate Action: Someone Goes Down

When a team member is hit, the first 10 seconds are critical. Your team's training and your clear orders determine whether someone gets saved or the situation gets worse.

## The Priority: SUPPRESS → TREAT → MOVE

When contact happens and someone is down:

### 1. SUPPRESS (First Priority)

Your first responsibility is not to help the casualty—it's to suppress the threat.

- **Everyone returns fire immediately.**
- **Establish suppressive fire** in the direction of the threat
- **Prevent the enemy from finishing off your casualty** or pinning your whole team
- **If you don't suppress, everything else fails** because more people get hit

As FTL, while your teams are engaging:

- "Green team, suppress that ridge line. Red team, I need you to shift fire west. Keep their heads down."
- Make sure fire is being returned effectively

### 2. TREAT (Second Priority)

Once suppressive fire is established and the immediate threat is contained:

- **Identify who is hit.** How bad is it? Can they fight? Can they move?
- **The buddy of the casualty provides immediate treatment.** This is their responsibility.
- **Other team members maintain suppressive fire and security.**
- **Do not try to carry someone to safety while still in contact.** You'll get more people hit.

### 3. MOVE (Third Priority)

Once the casualty is treated and the threat is suppressed:

- **Move the casualty to better cover if needed.**
- **Move the team away from the contact point** if possible
- **Coordinate with other teams if you need covering fire to move**

## Who Does What: Role Assignment

Make sure everyone understands their role if a casualty occurs.

- **Buddy immediately assists:** The buddy of the casualty provides initial response (tourniquet, bandage, etc.)
- **Medic responds:** Red moves to the casualty to take over treatment
- **Other team maintains security:** The unaffected color team provides covering fire and watches for threats, moving to the casualty if needed to help cover more effectively

## Calling for Help

When a casualty occurs, inform your OL immediately, then focus on the immediate situation:

"Atlas, this is Nova. Green has a casualty, we're under fire and managing it."

As the situation develops:
"Atlas, Nova. Green has one casualty, not serious. We're suppressing and treating. Will update."

Or if it's more serious:
"Atlas, Nova. Green has one casualty, urgent. We're suppressing and treating, but we need support."

Your OL needs to know:

- **How many casualties**
- **How serious** (urgent = immediate threat to life, can wait = treatable with time)
- **What you need** (extraction, medical support, fire support, etc.)

## Pre-Mission Preparation

Before the mission:

- **Identify who is trained/capable** of treating casualties
- **Ensure everyone has medical gear** (tourniquets, bandages, etc.)

## Common Casualty Mistakes

- **Panicking and leaving the casualty.** Your team stays together or they should.
- **Trying to extract under heavy fire** without suppression you'll get more people hit
- **Ignoring security** to focus on the casualty
- **Not communicating.** Other teams don't know if they should move, suppress, or wait
- **Waiting too long to call for help** when you can't handle the situation

## Remember

Casualty situations are high-stress. Your team looks to you to be calm and decisive. Even if you're uncertain, project confidence and give clear orders. That's what keeps people alive.
