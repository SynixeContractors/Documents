## Managing PMC Resources

As a PMC operator, you operate differently than military units. Resources aren't unlimited, missions are for profit, and efficiency matters. Resource management is part of your job as an FTL.

### Understanding Consumption

- **In contact:** You can burn through 200+ rounds per person in minutes when returning fire
- **Deliberate operations:** Controlled fire uses 10-30 rounds per engagement
- **Overspending:** Running low of ammo in the middle of a mission is a serious problem
- **Specialized equipment:** Grenades, AT rounds, medical supplies can be limited

### Ammo Shortages

If you're running low:

- **Conserve aggressively.** Controlled single shots only.
- **Shift to cover fire only.** Use terrain and suppression, not volume.
- **Consider repositioning or withdrawing** if you can't fight effectively

### Buddy Pair Ammunition Sharing

Within a buddy pair, ammo can be shared if one person is running critically low. However:

- Don't constantly shuffle ammo around
- Keep ammunition distributed so each person has enough for independent action
- If one person is burning ammo faster, address their technique or positioning

## Requesting Support

As an FTL, you're responsible for knowing what support is available and requesting it appropriately.

### Reinforcement

If your team is overwhelmed:

- **Request support early.** Don't wait until you're completely pinned.
- **Tell OL your situation clearly.** How many enemy? Position? Your team status?
- **Establish a rally point or blocking position** if withdrawing
- **Maintain contact until support arrives**

## Decision-Making: Fight vs. Conserve

You constantly balance effectiveness against resource conservation.

### When to Spend Resources Freely

- **Under heavy fire:** Return fire aggressively, conserve later
- **Defending teammates:** Use what you need to keep people alive
- **Mission-critical objective:** Sometimes you have to spend everything

### When to Conserve

- **Routine patrol:** Controlled fire, minimal ammo use
- **Early in mission:** You have 2-2.5 hours, don't burn through ammo in the first 30 minutes
- **Waiting for support:** Hold position, don't engage unless necessary

**Trust your judgment.** If the situation calls for aggressive action, act. If you have time, be conservative.

## Cost-Benefit Thinking (PMC Perspective)

As a PMC, this mission has to be profitable. Unnecessary losses or wasted equipment hurt the bottom line.

- **Casualties cost:** Dead contractors don't work next mission, medical care is expensive
- **Ammunition costs:** Wasted rounds are wasted money
- **Equipment damage:** Broken gear needs replacement
- **Mission failure:** No payment

This doesn't mean being cowardly. It means:
- **Execute the mission professionally.** Don't take unnecessary risks.
- **Protect your people.** A team that survives can work again.
- **Use resources efficiently.** Accomplish the objective without excess.
- **Make decisions that make sense financially.** Spend to save lives. Don't spend to look cool.

## Communicating Resource Status

Keep your OL informed of your resource status:

**Good:** "Command, Yellow is at 60% ammo, one minor casualty, ready to continue."

**Better:** "Command, Yellow status: 60% ammo, 1 minor casualty, continuing operations, holding position."

This lets OL plan and make decisions based on your actual situation, not assumptions.

## Pre-Mission Planning

Before you deploy:

- **Know your starting ammo and equipment** — this is what you have for the entire mission
- **Plan ammo consumption** for a 2-2.5 hour mission
- **Know the ROE and constraints** (can you use supports? Are there civvies?)
- **Understand the mission duration** and pace accordingly — manage your ammo from start to finish

Careful planning and ammunition discipline prevent running out of rounds in the middle of a firefight.
